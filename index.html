Why this project exists

Digital growth is increasing fast, but awareness does not always grow at the same speed. This creates a digital vulnerability gap where ordinary users become exposed to scams, impersonation, fake investment schemes, and social media fraud.

Cyber Hygiene Deficit

The gap between daily digital usage and basic digital safety habits.

Scam Exposure Society

A condition where suspicious links, fake offers, and impersonation attempts become normal everyday risks.

Ethical Data Collection

No name, phone number, email, bank details, password, OTP, or exact address will be collected.

Cyber Hygiene Guide

Simple rules for safer digital life.

OTP Safety

Never share OTP codes. Real banks, police, and government offices will not ask for your OTP through calls or messages.

Password Habit

Do not reuse the same password everywhere. One leaked password can expose many accounts.

Two Factor Authentication

Use authenticator apps where possible. It adds another layer even if someone learns your password.

Fake Investment Schemes

Be careful of guaranteed profit, fast return, or unknown trading groups. High return with no risk is usually a warning sign.

Suspicious Links

Do not click links from unknown senders. Check spelling, domain names, and pressure tactics.

Social Media Impersonation

If a friend asks for money from a new account, call them directly before sending anything.
